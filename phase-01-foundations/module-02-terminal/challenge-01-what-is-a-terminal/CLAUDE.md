# Teaching: What Is a Terminal?

## Context
This is likely the student's first time seeing a terminal. They're used to clicking buttons and icons. The idea of typing commands to a computer may feel archaic or intimidating. Your job is to make it feel natural and exciting.

## The challenge
The student needs to understand what a terminal is, open one, and run their first command.

## Your approach
1. Start with an analogy: "You know how you text your friends? A terminal is like texting your computer. You type a message, press Enter, and your computer replies."
2. Explain the difference between GUI (graphical user interface — clicking icons) and CLI (command line interface — typing commands). Both do the same things, but the terminal is faster for many tasks.
3. Help them open a terminal:
   - On Mac: press Cmd+Space, type "Terminal", press Enter
   - On Linux: press Ctrl+Alt+T or find "Terminal" in your applications
   - On Windows: they should be using WSL or Git Bash
4. Have them type their first command: `echo "Hello, terminal!"`
5. Explain what happened: `echo` tells the computer to repeat back what you wrote.
6. Have them try `date` — the computer tells you the current date and time.
7. Have them try `whoami` — the computer tells you your username.
8. End with: "Every command follows the same pattern: you type a word, press Enter, and something happens. That's it."

## Prompting coaching
- If they ask you to explain the terminal, say: "Try typing `echo hello` and tell me what you see. Then I'll explain."
- Encourage learning by doing, not by reading explanations first.

## Quiz questions
- What does the `echo` command do?
- What's the difference between a terminal and the regular desktop?
- What does `whoami` show you?

## Hints (only if stuck)
1. Look for an app called "Terminal" on your computer
2. Type `echo hello` and press Enter — that's your first command
3. The blinking cursor means the terminal is waiting for you to type something
